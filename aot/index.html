Natural Language Processing

In this analysis, I use the Penn Treebank Word Tokenizer to perform natural language processing on the dialogue in the manga, "Attack on Titan". Qualitative summary statistics such as word frequency, correlation, and averaging occured in the analysis.

Manga and Anime: Crossover Analysis

Retrieving the dialogue from the manga is significantly more complex computationally so I performed my analysis on the script from the anime. This choice was stricly based on the availability of the data. I read the manga and I am aware there is more content in the manga to analyze. Unfortunately, the qualitative data in this analysis is strictly what is being said in the anime.

Tokenized Data

The script is processed by assigning each word to a number. This way counts and correlations are easier to determine.

Term Document Matrix

A term document matrix is used specifically to understand the relationship words have to one another. This informationincludes what words appear near each other and the frequency that these combinations occur.

Word Frequency

This data is central to this analysis due to the type of analysis I am performing. The words that have the highest frequency generally paint a mental picture of the type of narrative that is being told.

Filtering

A big part of natural language processing is filtering characters such as (',",;,=,+,&,#,etc.) to ensure that words that include these characters are still included in the count, alongside the same words that occur without the problematic punctuation.

How is Attack on Titan making the reader feel?

This article is a mix between scholarly research, personal interpretation, and natural language processing. I will touch on all of these methods to ensure completeness and an unbias approach. Like many literary works, Attack on Titan is a rather interpretable piece of literature. There are many different interpretations and no one interpretation is the "right" one. Hajime Isayama (the author), may have the answers this research is attempting to uncover, but for now with the anime expected to conclude in the winter of 2022, Isayama is keeping a low profile in terms of giving away any information that may enter spoiler territory. With all of that being said, let's analyze away!!!

This is a dark story. The manga is drawn in black and white for a reason.

From the very beginning, it became clear that my research was not about to produce positive words. There is a sentiment analysis tactic called "Vader" processing that I would absolutely love to see done on the script of Attack on Titan. The vader sentiment analysis finds which words are considered positive and negative and counts them up. That is a clear next step for this research. However, even without Vader, I was capable of finding that there is little positivity to go around early on in the story. Below are the most common words across the scripts.

  • Wall(s)
  • Titan(s)
  • Humanity
  • Zhiganshina
  • Survive
  • Eaten
  • Government
  • Ugh
  • Survey Corps

Noting Names and their increased frequency in storytelling.

It is important to note that in any NLP analysis, that "stop words", (I, you, am, the, yes, no), are going to be the most frequent. Following stop words, names are also occuring at a heightened frequency. This is not an attempt to determine protagonists or character frequency so names are left out.

Stop words are not considered in this analysis. Computational limitations limited analysis to one anime episode script at a time. Each episode script is a near perfect representation of the manga in terms of Natural Language Processing. I read the manga to ensure accuracy and some concepts, especially religious connotations, are less represented in the anime script. If names were included in the dataset, Eren, Mikasa, Armin, etc. would immediately be the most frequent. This is a trend I see across most animes, where the characters say each other's names with varying levels of inflection. Comparing this to the manga yields interesting results. The manga had fewer instances of name repitition. Name frequency is interesting because in a hypothetical guessing game, "Name that manga/anime", the names of the characters and the top ten words listed above would be a pretty clear sign that the story being referenced is clearly Attack on Titan.

The story 10 words can tell

The bold words are the top ten words. Survey Corps are two words that appear in that order at a very hugh frequency so while they may appear seperate, they are usually grouped together.

If you had to write a story using only the words listed above you may have something that creates the same emotional feeling as Attack on Titan. The overwhelming frequency of the word wall or walls shows humanity's reliance on the wall surrounding Zhiganshina. The titans are essentially the opposition to humanity and the unique power or force of nature to this narrative. The survey corps is fighting back against the titans to keep the walls sealed and many of them get eaten by the titans in the process. Ultimately, this is story about humanity's government forming a survey corps that defends the walls from man-eating titans, ugh.
